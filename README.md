# Women's Heart Disease
This repository documents our progress in completing a personal project on evaluating the accuracy of heart disease diagnosis in women across different ancestries.

## Project Overview
Cardiovascular disease remains one of the leading causes of death among women worldwide. Polygenic risk scores (PRS) are increasingly used to estimate an individual's genetic risk for complex diseases, including heart disease. However, many genetic prediction models are trained using datasets dominated by individuals of European ancestry and are often underrepresentative of women. This imbalance may reduce predictive accuracy across other populations and contribute to disparities in cardiovascular risk assessment and diagnosis.

## Research Question
To what extent do current polygenic risk scores (PRS) accurately predict heart disease in women, and how does their performance vary across different ancestral groups and potential sources of diagnostic bias?
